Why this page exists

Better separation, faster re-entry.

The old page had the right content but the wrong pacing. Atlas turns the section into distinct lanes: how to choose a path, how to open the right version, and how to understand the supporting procedure layer around it.

Use pattern

Open the static flow when you need the whole sequence. Open the interactive path when you need branching, calculators, validations, or a guided work surface. Stay in procedures when the question is more about standards than steps.

How this section works

Three ways people actually use guided work.

The page is split so process steps, procedure standards, and quick re-entry signals do not blur together. That separation is what the old version was missing.

Standards, ownership, and the work around the flow.

The legacy section tracks procedures separately from walkthroughs. Atlas makes that split more useful by treating procedures as the rules-of-good-work layer instead of burying them as a leftover list item.

Procedure principles
01
Define the owner before the handoff.

Every procedure should make it obvious who carries the next move, who validates it, and where the proof of completion lives.

02
Separate validation from production momentum.

Teams move faster when the rule checks, exception handling, and sign-off points are visible instead of hidden in narrative copy.

03
Keep the procedure lane calmer than the flow lane.

Flows are for action. Procedures are for standards, thresholds, escalation paths, and the guardrails around the work.

Procedure theme

Intake and onboarding standards

Use procedures here to define the minimum starting package, required contacts, system readiness checks, and the sequence for getting a client from kickoff into active work.

Procedure theme

Validation and exception handling

Good procedure language should call out warning points clearly: what stops the process, what can proceed with sign-off, and what has to loop back for correction before the next lane opens.

Procedure theme

Closeout and evidence of completion

Atlas gives the procedure layer room to define what done means: confirmations, stored outputs, final checks, and the practical evidence someone else can rely on later.
